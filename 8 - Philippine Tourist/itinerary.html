Baguio, Benguet

DAY 1

Fresh air, pine scent, urban exploration commences!


Embrace Baguio's cool climate, vibrant markets, scenic parks, and rich cultural heritage. Explore the city's charm, nestled amidst lush mountains, and indulge in a memorable adventure in the heart of the Philippines.


Planned Trip:

  1. Arrival in Baguio and explore Session Road, public market, and Burnham Park.
  2. Discover attractions: Baguio Cathedral and Mines View Park.
  3. By night, have an overnight at hotels: Hotel Veniz, Venus Parkview Hotel, or Baguio Burnham Suites.

Burnham Park

Burnham Park, nestled in Baguio's heart, offers a serene escape amidst urban hustle. Spanning 32.84 hectares, it boasts verdant gardens, scenic ponds, and recreational facilities. Visitors enjoy paddle boating, leisurely strolls, or picnics, making it a beloved spot for relaxation and outdoor fun in Baguio, Philippines.

Baguio Cathedral

Baguio Cathedral, also known as Our Lady of Atonement Cathedral, stands majestically in the heart of Baguio City. Completed in 1936, its distinct pink fa√ßade and twin spires make it a landmark. Inside, visitors admire its grandeur and serene ambiance, often attending Mass or simply soaking in the tranquility amidst bustling Baguio.

Mines View Park

Mines View Park, situated in Baguio, Philippines, offers panoramic vistas of the city's old mining area. Visitors marvel at breathtaking views of mountains and valleys, shop for souvenirs, or enjoy horseback riding. It's a cultural hotspot, showcasing indigenous crafts and traditions, providing a unique blend of nature and heritage.

Veniz Hotel

Hotel Veniz, centrally located in Baguio, provides cozy and convenient accommodations for travelers. With comfortable rooms, friendly service, and proximity to attractions, it's a popular choice for those seeking comfort and affordability during their stay in the vibrant city of Baguio, Philippines.

Banaue, Ifugao

DAY 2

Embark on scenic journey to Banaue, marveling at nature's splendor!


Banaue captivates with its awe-inspiring UNESCO-listed rice terraces, carved into the Cordillera Mountains. Immerse in indigenous culture, scenic hikes, and panoramic views, making it a must-visit destination in the Philippines.


Planned Trip:

  1. Depart from Baguio to Banaue.
  2. Stop in Bontoc to visit the Bontoc Museum.
  3. Continue to Banaue and explore the Banaue Rice Terraces and Chico River.
  4. Visit Tappiya Falls in Batad.
  5. Overnight in Banaue: Banaue Hotel & Youth Hostel, Banaue Homestay, or Native Village Inn.

Bontoc Musuem

The Bontoc Museum, nestled in the Cordillera mountains, showcases cultural and historical artifacts of the indigenous peoples of the region. Through interactive exhibits and craft items, visitors uncover the fascinating history and cultural richness of the tribes in the area.

Rice Terraces & Chico River

Banaue's famed rice terraces, a UNESCO heritage, cascade down Cordillera Mountains, weaving into lush greenery. Adjacent, the Chico River meanders, adding to the picturesque landscape, creating a breathtaking vista in the Philippines.

Tappiya Falls

Tappiya Falls, nestled in Batad, Philippines, cascades majestically from towering cliffs into a serene pool below. Accessible via a scenic hike through lush rice terraces, it offers a refreshing retreat and a picturesque backdrop for nature enthusiasts and adventurers alike.

Native Village Inn

Native Village Inn, located in Banaue, offers rustic accommodations amidst stunning mountain vistas. Guests experience traditional Ifugao hospitality in cozy native huts while enjoying modern comforts. Surrounded by lush greenery and rice terraces, it's an ideal retreat for travelers seeking an authentic cultural experience in the Philippines.

Sagada, Mountain Province

DAY 3

Explore mystical caves, witness ancient burial rituals.


Renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, unique cultural traditions, and tranquil atmosphere. From mysterious caves to ancient burial rites, every corner of this region offers an immersive experience in the history and natural beauty of the Philippines.


Planned Trip:

  1. Depart from Banaue to Sagada.
  2. Explore Sumaguing and Lumiang Caves.
  3. Discover hanging coffins and ancient churches.
  4. Overnight in: Sagada Homestay or Sagada Igorot Inn.

Sumaguin Cave

Explore the depths of Sumaguing Cave, Sagada's renowned spelunking destination, filled with intricate limestone formations and underground rivers, offering an exhilarating adventure for thrill-seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Lumiang Cave

Adjacent to Sumaguing, captivates with its ancient burial chambers, adorned with stacked coffins, revealing Sagada's rich cultural heritage amidst stunning geological wonders.

Sagada Hanging Coffins

Perched on limestone cliffs, are a testament to the ancient burial traditions of the Igorot people, offering a glimpse into their spiritual beliefs and cultural practices.

Sagada Homestay

Experience genuine hospitality and immerse yourself in the local culture with a Sagada Homestay, offering cozy accommodations in traditional houses, warm hospitality, and authentic Ifugao cuisine, providing a memorable cultural experience in Sagada.

Baguio, Benguet

DAY 4

Experience majestic waterfalls, serene gardens, and scenic lakes in Baguio.


Immerse yourself in the natural wonders of Baguio. Discover the majestic beauty of Bomod-ok Falls, wander through the serene Botanical Garden, and enjoy leisurely boat rides amidst the scenic surroundings of Burnham Lake. Experience the enchanting charm of nature in this vibrant city.


Planned Trip:

  1. Depart from Sagada to Baguio.
  2. Stop to admire Bomod-ok Falls in Sagada.
  3. Return to Baguio and visit the Botanical Garden.
  4. Relax by Burnham Lake.
  5. At night, stay at: Hotel Veniz, Venus Parkview Hotel, or Baguio Burnham Suites.

Bomod-ok Falls

Bomod-ok Falls captivates with its majestic cascade, plunging into a crystal-clear pool amidst verdant foliage in Baguio. Its pristine waters and scenic surroundings offer a serene retreat for nature lovers, inviting exploration and awe-inspiring moments amidst the tranquil beauty of the Philippines.

Botanical Garden

Botanical Garden Baguio invites exploration of its tranquil sanctuary, showcasing diverse flora and fauna amidst lush landscapes. Visitors wander through winding paths, admiring vibrant floral displays and serene ponds, offering a peaceful retreat and immersive experience amidst the vibrant biodiversity of the Philippines.

Burnham Lake

Experience the enchanting ambiance of Burnham Lake at night in Baguio. Illuminated by shimmering lights, the tranquil waters reflect the city's skyline, creating a magical atmosphere. Enjoy leisurely strolls along the promenade, surrounded by the gentle rustle of trees and the soothing sounds of nature under the starry sky.

Baguio, Benguet

DAY 5

Memories cherished, adventures shared, until we meet again.


The Ibaloi Museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous Ibaloi people in Baguio. Through exhibits of artifacts, traditional clothing, and tools, visitors gain insights into their customs and way of life, preserving and celebrating the vibrant traditions of the Cordillera region.


Planned Trip:

  1. Enjoy a last morning in Baguio to explore remaining attractions or shop, and visit Ibaloi Museum.
