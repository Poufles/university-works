Ifugao Province, Philippines

Banaue Rice Terraces

Experience the awe-inspiring Banaue Rice Terraces, a UNESCO World Heritage site in the Philippines. Marvel at the ancient engineering marvels carved into the mountainsides, showcasing the rich cultural heritage and ingenuity of the Ifugao people. Don't miss this breathtaking natural wonder!

HISTORY

The Banaue Rice Terraces, often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," are ancient agricultural terraces carved into the mountains of Ifugao in the Philippines. Believed to be over 2,000 years old, these terraces were painstakingly built by hand by the indigenous Ifugao people using minimal tools and equipment.


Constructed without the use of mortar, the terraces are supported by stone walls that trap water from the forests above, creating a complex irrigation system that sustains the rice paddies throughout the dry season. This engineering marvel reflects the deep connection between the Ifugao people and their environment, as well as their ingenuity in adapting to the rugged terrain of the Cordillera region.

Come Visit!

The breathtaking Banaue Rice Terraces, sculpted into the mountainsides by the skilled hands of the Ifugao people, offer more than just their stunning natural beauty. These feats of ancient engineering are a testament to the harmonious relationship between humans and nature, with centuries of sustainable agricultural tradition behind them. Each terrace tells a story of perseverance, community, and respect for the environment, with cultivation techniques passed down through generations. These awe-inspiring landscapes are not only a visual delight but also a source of inspiration and reverence for the ancestral wisdom of the indigenous peoples of the Philippines. A visit to the Banaue Rice Terraces provides travelers with a unique opportunity to connect with the history, culture, and nature of this iconic region, and to immerse themselves in a way of life that has endured through the ages.

Baguio, Philippines

Mines View Park

Dive into the history and natural beauty of Mines View Park in Baguio, offering a spectacular view of mountain mines, artisanal souvenirs, and a tranquil outdoor ambiance. Discover this picturesque gem of the Philippines, where the past meets the present in a breathtaking landscape.

HISTORY

Nestled in the highlands of Baguio, Philippines, Mines View Park is a living testament to the region's mining heritage. In the early 20th century, Baguio was a favored destination for American settlers due to its cool climate and picturesque landscapes. Mining played a crucial role in the region's development, with abundant gold and silver mines in the surrounding mountains.


Mines View Park derives its name from its impressive panoramic view of the surrounding mountain mines. Over the years, it has become an iconic spot for locals and visitors alike, offering not only a spectacular view but also a unique cultural experience. Local artisans sell handmade souvenirs, and visitors can have their photos taken in traditional Filipino attire with the indigenous horses known as 'Kalesa.'

Come Visit!

Immerse yourself in the captivating beauty and rich history of Mines View Park in Baguio. As you step into this enchanting haven nestled amidst the verdant mountains, you'll be greeted by a breathtaking panoramic vista that stretches as far as the eye can see. The air is crisp, carrying whispers of bygone eras when mining was the lifeblood of this region.


But it's not just the views or the cultural immersion that make Mines View Park a must-visit destination. It's the sense of connection to the land, to its history, and to the resilient spirit of its people. It's a place where every corner tells a story, where the past dances harmoniously with the present.


So, come, wander through the winding paths, breathe in the mountain air, and let Mines View Park weave its magic around you. Discover the beauty, the heritage, and the serenity that await amidst the towering peaks and lush landscapes of Baguio's beloved treasure.

Mountain Province, Philippines

Sagada Hanging Coffins

Embark on a journey through time as you explore this sacred site, where each coffin tells a silent story of reverence and remembrance. Learn about the fascinating rituals and customs surrounding the hanging coffins, from the intricate process of carving the wooden caskets to the spiritual significance of their elevated placement.

HISTORY

Dating back over a thousand years, it is deeply rooted in the cultural traditions of the Igorot people of the Philippines. This ancient burial practice involves suspending coffins from the sides of limestone cliffs, a custom believed to have originated from the Igorots' desire to be closer to their ancestral spirits and to protect their deceased loved ones from animals and floods.


The Sagada Hanging Coffins are believed to have originated from ancient burial practices of the indigenous tribes in Sagada's mountainous regions. The exact origins remain mysterious, but local folklore suggests that placing the coffins higher ensures a smoother journey to the afterlife for the deceased. Traditionally, skilled craftsmen hand-carve the coffins from solid wood, which are then meticulously placed in natural crevices or attached to wooden stakes driven into the cliff faces. This process requires great precision to securely anchor the coffins against the elements and the test of time.

Come Visit!

Imagine standing at the foot of these towering cliffs, surrounded by the whispers of ancient rituals and the gentle rustle of the wind through the trees. As you gaze upon these weathered wooden coffins, intricately hand-carved by skilled craftsmen, you can't help but feel a profound sense of reverence for the ancestors who rest peacefully in their embrace.


So, whether you're a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or simply seeking a glimpse into the soul of the Philippines, the Sagada Hanging Coffins await your discovery. Join me as we uncover the secrets of this ancient burial ground and forge a connection to the past that will stay with you long after you've returned home.

Mountain Province, Philippines

Sumaguing Cave

Traverse through winding passages and cavernous chambers, guided by the soft glow of your torchlight. Along the way, you'll encounter shimmering underground pools, hidden waterfalls, and mysterious rock formations that will leave you spellbound.

HISTORY

Sumaguing Cave, also known as the Big Cave, holds a significant place in the cultural and geological history of the Philippines. Carved over millions of years by the relentless forces of water and time, this magnificent limestone cave system is believed to be one of the oldest in the country.


Throughout history, Sumaguing Cave has been revered by the indigenous peoples of the Cordillera region, who consider it a sacred and mystical place. It served as a sanctuary for rituals and ceremonies, with ancient artifacts and rock formations bearing witness to the rituals of the past.


The name 'Sumaguing' is derived from the local dialect, meaning 'to walk carefully', a testament to the challenging terrain and the respect that locals have for the cave's natural wonders. For generations, it has been a source of fascination and inspiration for explorers, scientists, and adventurers alike, drawing visitors from far and wide to marvel at its beauty.

Come Visit!

Get ready for an extraordinary experience as you venture into Sumaguing Cave, a geological wonder hidden within the mountains of the Cordillera. Our team is here to guide you through a fascinating network of underground passages, where every corner reveals a new natural marvel.


Sumaguing Cave is more than just a cave - it's a journey through time, where you can uncover the millennia-old secrets of earth formation. Accompanied by our experienced guides, you'll traverse rocky tunnels, descend into mysterious chasms, and marvel at breathtaking stalactite and stalagmite formations.


Whether you're an intrepid adventurer or a nature lover seeking new discoveries, Sumaguing Cave offers an unparalleled experience. Come and create unforgettable memories in the heart of nature, where every step brings you closer to the hidden wonders of our planet.
